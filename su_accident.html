Crash sites of pedestrian and cyclist near Seattle University from 2022


The data is Seattle Crash data from 2022 from all weather and road conditions, focusing on pedestrian and cyclist accident types. The map uses symbol encodings: a person and a bicycle as markers to distinguish these types. The color encodings represent the number of people who got severely injured in the accidents, with the scheme from green to red, as more red is perceived as more blood and higher in severity. Tooltips reveal the accident type and number of severed injuries, which were encoded with symbols and color. The title conveys the context of the interactive map in text encodings, giving context to what the map is for. The map starts with Seattle University in the center, with the SU logo for reference. This start could be exported to a static print and still points out many crash locations in Seattle University surroundings. However, the visual itself is less effective in static print since few pieces of information are encoded as tooltips without legends.
